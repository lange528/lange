"The Catch" This action scene depicts the heated excitement of Super Bowl XLIV in South Florida while playing up the NFL slogan, “Own the Moment”.
"I-35 Showdown" This match up graphic highlights the proximity of two NBA teams located on Interstate 35. It was designed as part of an inaugural season series celebrating the Oklahoma City Thunder’s first season as a NBA franchise.
"Trading Card" This Super Bowl XLIV graphic replicates the feel of a vintage football trading card. The kicker featured in this design extends beyond the card’s edges, preventing this one from being too “in-the-box”.
"Go Green" An organically stylized rendering of the typeface used in Reebok’s new wordmark. Brown branches and leaf-like brush strokes play up Reebok’s “Go Green” initiative.
"Cavs Beat LA" This modified Cleveland Cavaliers logo emphasizes the significance of a potential match up between two of the NBA’s most exciting teams. A small team logo is included to cure the graphic, in addition to the 2009 Finals logo. These elements help balance the composition while providing context for the event.
"Magic Beat LA" The phrase “Beat LA” has been prevalent in recent years because of the Laker’s dominance. This execution of the phrase uses the same typeface found in the primary logo for the Orlando Magic. This graphic highlights the importance of the match up and what is at stake, the Larry O’Brien Championship trophy.
"Goal Post Stripe" A discovery piece that features a key element of the Super Bowl XLIV logo, the goal post. The digital feel created by the goal post pattern adds visual interest to this graphic.
"Keystone Koldwar" Russian inspired design pitting two NHL rivals against each other in a dueling layout. The allusion to the Cold War and the employment of two helmets adds emphasis to the war theme. The Keystone shaped background element ties into this heated rivalry in the state of Pennsylvania.
"Super Bowl XLIII Head to Head" This graphic is built around a lock-up shape similar to that of the Super Bowl XLIII logo while highlighting two star players in a potential match up. “The Big Game” phrase repeats in the background to provide added texture and depth to the overall composition.
"Pro Bowl Poster" The intent of this art deco inspired piece is to relate to the architecture found in South Florida. The theatre marquee draws attention to one of the NFL’s more overlooked events.
"WNBA Locker Room" This WNBA Locker Room graphic is composed to break away from a traditional symmetrical approach. It emphasizes the ultimate goal with the inclusion of the WNBA Championship trophy. The italicized typeface and the trophy’s perspective convey a sense of speed and movement.
"Madhouse On Madison" This graphic celebrates the “Madhouse On Madison”, home of the Chicago Blackhawks, with a threshold image of the United Center. The typeface in this design is similar to that of the illuminated sign found inside the building.
"Logo Play" For added authenticity, this unique player design is built with the same shape and style as the team’s preferred logo. A smaller team logo is included below to cure the graphic.
"Let's Go Heat" A best seller for the Heat’s playoff campaign, this design plays off the team’s “White Hot” theme.
"Front and Center" This Western Conference Finals participant graphic for the Dallas Mavericks uses drop shadows and a halftone rendering of the team logo to provide added dimension to an otherwise flat design.
"Fire It Up" A 2011 Camaro was modified in Photoshop to create a custom sports car for this threshold graphic. The resulting image is complemented with aggressive type that has the appearance of fresh tire marks.
"Big City NBA" This design combines a generic skyline halftone image with an overlaying team logo. The oversized city graphic is intended to hook to the urban culture associated with the NBA.
"Stanley Cup Final Roster" A Stanley Cup Final participant graphic, this design lists every player on the active roster for the Boston Bruins championship run.
"Kurt Warner's Retirement" This design commemorates Kurt Warner’s twelve year career as one of the NFL’s elite quarterbacks. The threshold image of Warner on the front interacts with a distressed Cardinals logo, while the back lists accomplishments from his historic career as one of the league’s most respected players.
"The Finals MVP" This graphic celebrates Dirk Nowitzki’s 2011 Finals MVP achievement. The design combines the authentic team font with tonal and colorized player images.
